I'm currently [researching](https://blog.martinssamuel.com/my-research) misinforamtion and disinformation using machine learning. These are kinds of deceptive information commonly called _fake news_, though I prefer the term [*Information Disorder*](https://firstdraftnews.org/en/education/curriculum-resource/information-disorder-useful-graphics). I recently publised an exploratory [paper](https://blog.martinssamuel.com/exploring-thematic-coherence-in-fake-news) on the current direction of my research. If you're interested, I'm curating information and tools for researching and learning about fake news in [this](https://github.com/m-arti/infodis) Github repository. I'm compiling some of them in this note.

## Resources

- [**Information disorder research**](https://github.com/m-arti/infodis/blob/master/Information%20Disorder%20Research.md): resources for research on various kinds of information disorder, such as fake news, rumours and satire.

- [**News and media literacy**](https://github.com/m-arti/infodis/blob/master/News%20and%20Media%20Literacy.md): resources for teaching and learning news and media literacy.

- [**Podcasts and talks**](https://soundcloud.com/martinssamuel/sets/infodis): if you prefer listening to reading, here are some interesting talks and conversations on the matter.
